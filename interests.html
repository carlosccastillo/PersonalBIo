Interests

There isn’t a single thing this young man doesn’t enjoy. From the diversity of foods to the complexity of evaluating Stoke’s Theorem, Carlos enjoys everything.

It all started at age 4 with the introduction of soccer. It was the first sight, first feel, first sound and evidently the first taste of fresh cut grass combined with a soccer ball that sparked his interest for a beautiful game. Soccer is his safe haven. Most commonly referred to his “go-to” stress reliever, there isn’t a day soccer is not on his mind. Some referred to Carlos as “Formica Atomica”, more commonly known as the “Atomic Ant”. Despite only being 5 feet 4 inches, Carlos is known to be an explosive ant on the pitch. He uses his creativity and skills to silence those who think size matters. Off the pitch Carlos admires watching and learning more about soccer by observing moves or game matches or individuals who speak thegame of football. Soccer is his life.

During his childhood, Carlos also competed in baseball, basketball, cross-country, track and field, and ultimate frisbee. Deeply in love with sports and its thrill, Carlos ran hisfirst LA Marathon at the age of 14, coming in 2nd place for the 18 and under group. “It was a tough journey but my mind was set and I never gave up. I love it. I just kept pushing on”, exclaimed Carlos during an interview following up the event.

Academically, Carlos is always enthusiastic to indulge in math studies. Like programming, math is a just another complex language. A complex language should be studied and admired. He takes this deep mindset of math from his influentially Calculus professor back at the Ranch. By the end of his high school years, Carlos felt he was prepared to further his education at a university.

On November 19, 2015, Carlos took a new adventure. This adventure could potentially be a life or death moment but he embraced the wild side. Carlos went snowboarding with his brother forthe first time. They hit the huge slopes of Big Bear. What a view those mountains portray. This became known to be one of Carlos’ most enjoyable hobby to this day.

Friends and family always come first.

Carlos enjoys the little things in life. From a nice walk along the beach to rock climbing in the Los Angeles National Forest, there isn’t a single thing he doesn’t enjoy. Among various activities to do, a simple hike through the Los AngelesNational Forest relaxing to Tame Impala, his favorite chill artist. His choice of music depends on the situation, although he definitely refrains from listening to country music and depressing music.
